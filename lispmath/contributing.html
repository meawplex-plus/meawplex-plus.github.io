Contributing to lispmath

When I say "contribute", what do you think I mean?

I don't mean:

  • Changing important files for no reason (i.e. the .gitignore)
  • Saying ruid/unnecessary things about others' work; "This will never work!!" is an example of this, also "Don't put that there!" in no context is an example.
  • Being violent about who does what work and who will do what when, etc. People have their own schedules!!

I do mean:

  • Helping people doing hard work to modify files / create new ones
  • Making new files / modding them yourself!!
  • Finding issues and fixing them, where you can.

Here are our rules and guidelines.

  1. Do not mod the .gitignore unless your computer is giving files in pushes that are not supposed to be in there; if it is, notify us and tell us what is being included wrongly.
  2. Respect others. If someone is modding a file, as mentioned earlier here, don't say hurtful things to them!
  3. When you create a file, make sure everyone knows why in your pull request.
  4. Don't use pull requests constantly. Put large groups of files in one large pull, not 30 separate ones.
  5. No strong language. None of it. If it is used, you will possibly be blocked from the contrib.
  6. Have fun!! And... stay a Lisper!
